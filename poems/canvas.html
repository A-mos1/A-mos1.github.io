Canvas

On our way back from little tibet,

we walk past a large blank canvas on the curb.

It’s vacant crevices beg us to take it home,

to create something together on warped edges that stretch past the horizon,

Maybe that happens in another universe,

but in this one,

you keep                                                  walking.

I can’t carry it alone.

The canvas remains blank,

untouched and unlived.
